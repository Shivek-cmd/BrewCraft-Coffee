## What Makes Cold Brew Different?

Cold brew isn't just iced coffee. It's a completely different extraction method — coffee grounds steeped in cold water for 18 to 24 hours, without any heat involved. The result? A smooth, low-acid, naturally sweet concentrate that's unlike anything you can get from a hot brew poured over ice.

At [BrewCraft Coffee](/), we've spent years perfecting our cold brew recipe. This guide gives you everything we know.

## The Right Coffee Ratio

The golden ratio for cold brew concentrate is **1:4 — 1 part coffee to 4 parts water** by weight.

For a 1-litre batch:
- 250g coarsely ground coffee
- 1000ml cold filtered water

If you want ready-to-drink (not concentrate), use 1:8 instead.

## Grind Size Matters

Use a **coarse grind** — similar to raw sugar crystals. Fine grinds over-extract during the long steep, leading to bitter, muddy cold brew. If you're using our [Fresh Roasted Coffee Beans](/services/fresh-roasted-coffee-beans), set your grinder to the coarsest setting.

## The 18-Hour Steep

1. Combine coffee and water in a large jar or French press
2. Stir gently to ensure all grounds are saturated
3. Cover and refrigerate for **18–24 hours**
4. Filter through a fine mesh strainer, then again through a paper filter
5. Store in a sealed glass bottle — stays fresh for up to 14 days

## Serve It Right

Pour over ice, dilute 1:1 with water or milk if using concentrate, and never heat it — you'll destroy the smooth character that makes cold brew special.

## Final Thoughts

Cold brew is one of the most forgiving brew methods once you understand the basics. The long, cold extraction forgives inconsistent grind sizes and gives you a wide window for experimentation. Start with our ratio, then adjust to your taste.
