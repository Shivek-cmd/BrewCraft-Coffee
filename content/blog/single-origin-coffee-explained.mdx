## What Does Single-Origin Actually Mean?

Single-origin coffee comes from one specific farm, region, or country — not a blend of beans from multiple locations. When you buy a bag labeled "Ethiopia Yirgacheffe," every bean in that bag came from that specific region of Ethiopia.

Contrast this with commercial blends, which combine beans from multiple countries to achieve a consistent, predictable flavour profile. Blends are reliable. Single-origin coffees are *interesting*.

## Why the Origin Matters So Much

Coffee, like wine, is profoundly shaped by where it grows. The combination of altitude, soil, rainfall, temperature, and processing method creates what coffee professionals call *terroir* — the unique character of a place expressed in the cup.

This is why:
- **Ethiopian coffees** tend to have bright, floral, tea-like qualities with fruit notes
- **Colombian coffees** offer balanced sweetness, medium body, and caramel-like flavours
- **Indonesian coffees** (Sumatra, Java) are earthy, full-bodied, and low-acid
- **Kenyan coffees** are intensely fruity, often blackcurrant or tomato-like, with bold acidity

None of these are better or worse — they're just different experiences.

## Choosing Your First Single-Origin

**If you like milk in your coffee:** Start with Brazil or Colombia — both have enough body and sweetness to shine through milk.

**If you drink black coffee:** Try Ethiopia or Kenya — the complexity and fruit notes are best appreciated without dairy.

**If you want something unexpected:** Go for a Yemeni or Ecuadorian lot — rare, complex, and genuinely surprising.

## The Freshness Factor
